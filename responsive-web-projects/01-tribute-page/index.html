Kuppali Venkatappa Puttappa

The great kannada poet of the 20th century

Here's a time line of Kuvempu's life:

  • 1904 - Born in Kuppalli, a village in Shivamogga district of the erstwhile Kingdom of Mysore
  • 1929 - Pursued college studies at the Maharaja College of Mysore and graduated in 1929, majoring in Kannada
  • 1929 - Began his academic career as a lecturer of Kannada language at the Maharaja's College in Mysore
  • 1936 - Wrote his first novel - Kannuru Hegadathi
  • 1937 - Married Hemavathi
  • 1949 - Composed Sri Ramayana Darshana the most popular work and the magnum opus in Kannada
  • 1960 - Got retired as the Vice-Chancellor of Mysore University
  • 1967 - Earned Jnanapeeth award
  • 1987 - The Government of Karnataka started the Kuvempu University
  • 1994 - Dies at the age of 90

-- Kuvempu
